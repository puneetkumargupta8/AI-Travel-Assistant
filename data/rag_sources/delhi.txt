Delhi, the capital of India, is a vibrant metropolis that seamlessly blends historical landmarks with modern infrastructure. The city is divided into Old Delhi and New Delhi, each offering distinct experiences to visitors.

Old Delhi, established by the Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in 1639, features narrow winding lanes, bustling markets, and Mughal-era monuments. The iconic Red Fort, Jama Masjid, and Chandni Chowk are located here. The Red Fort, built in 1648, served as the main residence of the Mughal emperors. Jama Masjid, completed in 1656, is one of the largest mosques in India.

New Delhi, designed by British architects Edwin Lutyens and Herbert Baker, was officially inaugurated in 1931. It features wide boulevards, government buildings, and parks. Notable landmarks include India Gate, Rashtrapati Bhavan, Parliament House, and Humayun's Tomb. Humayun's Tomb, built in 1570, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and inspired the construction of the Taj Mahal.

The city experiences a semi-arid climate with extreme temperatures. Summers are scorching hot (April-June) while winters are quite cold (December-February). The best time to visit Delhi is from October to March when the weather is pleasant.

Delhi's cuisine reflects its diverse cultural heritage. Street food is particularly famous, with popular items including chaat, paranthas at Paranthe Wali Gali, kebabs, and biryani. Dilli Haat is a craft park where tourists can shop for handicrafts from different parts of India.

The city is well-connected by air, rail, and road. Indira Gandhi International Airport serves as a major aviation hub. The Delhi Metro provides efficient public transportation connecting major tourist attractions. Auto-rickshaws and cabs are readily available for local transport.

Major festivals celebrated in Delhi include Diwali, Holi, Eid, and Christmas. The city comes alive during these festivals with decorations, special foods, and cultural events.

Popular shopping destinations include Connaught Place, Sarojini Nagar Market, Greater Kailash, and Chandni Chowk. Each market offers different products ranging from branded goods to traditional crafts and textiles.

The city houses several museums including the National Museum, National Gallery of Modern Art, and Crafts Museum. These institutions showcase India's rich cultural heritage, art, and history.

Tourist tips for visiting Delhi include staying hydrated, being cautious of air pollution, bargaining at markets, and dressing modestly when visiting religious sites. It's advisable to carry a mask during winter months due to air pollution.
